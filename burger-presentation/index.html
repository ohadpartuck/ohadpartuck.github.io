you are having trouble keeping up with the work load

ideas?

More employees.

Split Tasks.

Hire a cook to work without keeping the client waiting.

Pass the orders to him on an orders clipper.

Now, Let's say

that your clients like to order what they previously ordered

So you keep a orders directory in the kitchen

But, the cooks just stack the orders one by one so it's organized by order time

a specific client comes and wants what he ordered has last time, now what?

Let's make a list that sorts the orders by the client phone number and keeps the location of the orders.

Awesome. The clients love it.

Now you have 10 cooks cooking for your clients :-(

The run into each other when trying to get the clients orders

Let's make another 3 directories where we store our clients orders

How can we improve this even more?

I know

We'll make a note. We'll list our popular clients order history.

We'll put the note next to the girls (or guys) taking the orders.

That way, they won't have to bother the cooks with looking for the order history.

Great. We did it again.

Adding another layer

Our busy workers are overloaded sometimes.

And nobody cares.

Let's hire a consultant.

We'll tell him to check our place every two hours.

and scream out if he notices something weird, like overloaded employees.

We value our workers' input

Let's put an employee complaint box.

If it gets too full, then are workers are not happy.

We have to take care of them, or they'll quit.

How to scale up your web application

This time, really. An actual web application

Let's say it's an app to manage a burger place

Let's say

You build a nice application

you do it all by your self

and let's say it's successful!

you are having trouble keeping up with the work load

ideas?

More servers.

SOA.

Service Orient Architecture

Async workers.

Via a message broker.

Now, Let's say

that your clients like to order what they previously ordered

So you keep a orders directory in the database

But, the workers just stack the orders one by one so it's organized by order time

a specific client comes and wants what he ordered has last time, now what?

Let's make a index that sorts the orders by the client phone number and keeps the location of the orders.

Awesome. The clients love it.

Now you have 10 workers working for your clients :-(

The run into each other when trying to get to the database

Let's do a master/slave configuration

How can we improve this even more?

I know

We'll save our popular clients order history in the cache.

We'll put in one server for the application responsible for taking the orders.

That way, they won't have to bother the DB with looking for the order history.

Great. We did it again.

Adding another layer

Our busy servers and processes are overloaded sometimes.

And nobody cares.

Let's install a monitoring software.

We'll tell it to check our place every two seconds.

and scream out if it notices something weird, like overloaded servers.

We value our servers' input

Let's install central error log.

If it gets too full, then are servers are not overloaded.

We have to take care of them, or they'll crash.

That's all folks.
